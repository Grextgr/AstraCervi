About the team

Where are we from?

We are a bilingual team (English–Spanish) from Hermosillo, Sonora, Mexico, participating in the 2025 NASA Space Apps Challenge.

Team

Our team is formed by four Industrial Engineering students from Instituto Tecnológico de Hermosillo (ITH) and one Mechatronics Engineering student from Tecnológico de Monterrey (TEC).

Why did we choose this challenge?

We chose this challenge because in Mexico, over 30% of children lack access to interactive or digital reading materials, missing the chance to explore, imagine, and learn about their world.

Our problem

As digital education grows, this gap continues to expand—leaving more children without access to educational content that inspires curiosity and understanding.

Our solution

To address this, we created AstraStories: a digital platform with illustrated, interactive stories that explain complex topics like space weather in a fun and accessible way.

Our mission

Our mission is to turn reading into a tool for equity, imagination, and discovery, helping children grow with knowledge, empathy, and wonder for the universe they live in.

ABOUT THE CHALLENGE

Stellar Stories

Although the Sun is 93 million miles away from our planet, solar activity can significantly impact our daily lives. “Space weather”—the variations that occur in the space environment between the Sun and Earth—can impact technologies in space and on Earth. Your challenge is to write and illustrate a digital children’s story that explains what space weather is and the varying impacts it has on different people such as farmers, pilots, astronauts, power grid operators, and the general public. You can tell the story from the perspective of a person impacted by space weather or from the point of view of a solar flare or coronal mass ejection (CME) as it approaches Earth.

PROJECT ASTRASTORIES

Project Summary

AstraStories is a digital platform that brings illustrated and interactive stories to children, making science and reading accessible to everyone. In Mexico, millions of children grow up without access to digital reading materials, limiting their imagination and curiosity. AstraStories turns this challenge into an opportunity by offering fun and educational stories that explain complex scientific topics like space weather. Through its freemium, premium, and school models, the project promotes reading comprehension, scientific curiosity, and critical thinking, turning each story into a bridge between imagination and knowledge, helping children dream without limits and discover the wonders of the universe they live in.

Project Details

AstraStories is a digital platform that promotes reading and scientific curiosity among children through illustrated and interactive stories about topics like space weather, science, and values. Designed with a hybrid model (free, premium, and school) it ensures access for all children, families, and educational institutions. By turning reading into an engaging and inclusive experience, AstraStories fosters imagination, comprehension, and equity, helping every child discover the joy of learning and the wonder of the universe.

Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Using AI tools such as ChatGPT and Gemini, we create engaging, educational, and accessible content. By turning reading into an inclusive experience, AstraStories fosters imagination, comprehension, and equity, helping every child discover the joy of learning and the wonder of the universe.
